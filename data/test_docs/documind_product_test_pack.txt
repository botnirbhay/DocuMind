Section 1: Program Overview

Project Atlas is an internal claims automation initiative for regional insurance teams.
The current rollout phase began on March 4, 2026.
Atlas is owned by the Operations Enablement group.
The program sponsor is Mira Shah.
The implementation lead is Daniel Reed.

Section 2: Support and Availability

Standard support hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM IST.
Critical incident support is available every day from 6:00 AM to 11:00 PM IST.
Requests submitted outside standard support hours are queued for the next support window unless they are marked critical.

Section 3: Review and Appeal Timelines

Initial claim reviews are completed within 5 business days.
Appeal reviews are completed within 7 business days.
Escalated fraud-related appeals may take up to 12 business days.
If required evidence is missing, the review clock pauses until the missing material is received.

Section 4: Refund and Fee Rules

Platform setup fees are non-refundable once account provisioning has started.
Monthly subscription charges are refundable only if the customer reports a service-blocking issue within 10 calendar days of renewal.
Approved refunds are processed within 5 business days after confirmation.
Charge disputes must be submitted through the billing portal.

Section 5: Eligibility Requirements

Customers must maintain an active enterprise plan to use Atlas workflow automations.
Each customer workspace must have at least one verified administrator.
Single sign-on is recommended but not mandatory.
Sandbox environments are available only for teams with more than 50 active users.

Section 6: Upload and Document Handling

The platform accepts PDF, DOCX, and TXT files.
The maximum upload size is 25 MB per file.
Scanned image-only PDFs are not guaranteed to produce reliable text extraction.
Uploaded source files are retained for 30 days unless the workspace is cleared earlier.

Section 7: Security and Retention

Session memory should never be treated as a source of truth over the uploaded documents.
Generated answers must rely only on retrieved document context.
Evidence logs are retained for 14 days for evaluation and debugging.
Uploaded files are removed immediately when a workspace reset is triggered by the user.

Section 8: Named Teams and Contacts

Billing issues are handled by the Revenue Operations team.
Security review questions should be sent to the Trust Engineering team.
Program communications are coordinated by Rina Patel.
Release approvals require sign-off from Mira Shah and the platform compliance reviewer.

Section 9: Product Limits

Each workspace supports up to 200 uploaded documents at one time.
Search requests return the top matching chunks rather than full documents.
Confidence labels are shown as Strong grounding, Low confidence, or Insufficient context.

Section 10: What Is Out of Scope

Atlas does not provide legal advice.
Atlas does not make hiring decisions.
Atlas does not perform medical diagnosis.
Atlas does not replace a formal fraud investigation process.

Section 11: Example Workflow

Step 1: Upload source documents.
Step 2: Prepare the workspace for search.
Step 3: Ask a grounded question.
Step 4: Open the citations and verify the source text.
